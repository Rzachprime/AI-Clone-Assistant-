## Problem Section

### Header

Why AI still feels inconsistent for most people

### Body

The problem is often not the model.

It is the missing context.

Without enough context, AI guesses.

It gives generic advice, misses hidden constraints, and forgets what matters from one moment to the next.

That is why even good prompts can still feel unreliable.

## 3-Level Section

### Header

The three levels of building a useful clone

### Cards

#### Level 1: Prompt Engineering

Learn how to ask clearly, define the role, request the right format, and improve the quality of the response.

#### Level 2: Context Sandwich

Learn how to surround a prompt with the situation, the background, the request, and the constraints so AI can respond in a way that actually fits.

#### Level 3: Context Engineering

Learn how to capture and reuse context from transcripts, notes, decisions, calendars, and recurring workflows so the clone can become more proactive over time.

## Passive-to-Active Section

### Header

Where Context Engineering starts

### Body

It usually begins with a passive use case.

You record a meeting.

You transcribe it.

You ask AI to summarize the discussion, extract action items, and prepare follow-ups.

That alone creates value.

But once that context is available consistently, the clone can begin helping in more active ways.

It can prepare tomorrow's planning context, collect open loops, organize recurring notes, and surface what needs attention before it gets buried.
